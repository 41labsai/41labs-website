Self-serve platform or done-for-you agent

AiSensy starts cheap. Interakt has a free plan. 41 Closer starts at S$690 a month. If price were the whole story, this article would be one line long.

Here is what the pricing pages do not show. A self-serve platform is a box of parts. Someone in your company has to connect the WhatsApp Business API, build the campaigns and flows, write every reply, switch on the AI add-on, watch the inbox daily, and fix what breaks. Then keep improving it every month, because the first version is never right.

If you have that person, self-serve is genuinely good value. Hundreds of thousands of businesses run AiSensy and Interakt well, and both give you a lot of capability for the money.

If you do not have that person, a familiar pattern plays out. The tool gets half set up over a free weekend. The bot learns to answer three FAQs. Then a customer asks a real question, the bot has no answer, and your staff go back to typing replies by hand. The subscription keeps billing. Nobody cancels it, and nobody uses it.

That is not a flaw in AiSensy or Interakt. It is the adoption pattern of every self-serve tool, from CRMs to email software. A done-for-you agent flips the model. 41 Labs builds it, runs it, and tunes it, and you review results instead of building flows. So the honest question is not which platform is best. It is who in your company will run it.

When AiSensy or Interakt is the better choice

Plenty of businesses should buy the platform, not the service. Be honest with yourself about which list you are on.

  • Your budget is tight and you want to start cheap. Interakt has a free Starter plan and paid WhatsApp plans from US$55 a month. AiSensy offers a 14-day free trial and low monthly pricing. 41 Closer starts at S$690 a month and cannot match that entry price, and I will not pretend otherwise.
  • You want WhatsApp marketing and broadcasts at scale. AiSensy is built for this. Broadcasts, campaign templates, and an AI Ads Manager are its home turf, and it says 210,000+ businesses use it.
  • You want WhatsApp and Instagram in one place. Interakt covers both, plus a support hub and a light sales CRM, so a small team can run marketing, support, and pipeline from one login. It is backed by Jio Haptik, part of Reliance Jio.
  • You have a capable person to run it. Someone who enjoys building flows, testing them, and improving them monthly. In those hands, both platforms return far more than they cost.
  • Your use case is simple. Answering the same ten FAQs, sending order updates, or broadcasting a monthly promo. No-code flows handle this well. You do not need a managed sales agent for it.
  • You want to keep everything in-house. Self-serve means you own the account, the data, and the day-to-day, with no outside team involved.

If two or more of those describe you, buy the platform. It is the right call, and you will not miss what 41 Closer does. For the wider field of options, see our guide to the best AI chatbots for business in Singapore.

Questions to ask before you choose

Run any WhatsApp AI option, including 41 Closer, through this short checklist before you sign anything.

  • Who on my team will connect the WhatsApp Business API and keep it running? If the honest answer is nobody, a self-serve platform will sit unused.
  • Is the AI included, or is it a paid add-on? On both AiSensy and Interakt the AI agent is an add-on on top of the base subscription, so add that cost in.
  • What will WhatsApp per-message charges add on top? Marketing, utility, and authentication messages are billed separately from the software on every platform here.
  • How fast do we answer enquiries today, and what does a slow reply cost us? Put a dollar figure on the leads that go cold after hours.
  • Do we need broadcasts and marketing, or enquiries answered and closed? The first points to AiSensy or Interakt. The second points to a managed agent.
  • If it breaks or the flows go stale, who fixes it, us or the vendor? With self-serve it is you. With a managed service it is the provider.
  • Is one closed deal worth hundreds or thousands of dollars? If yes, speed of reply matters more than the subscription price.
