Self-serve tool vs managed agent: which do you need?

Every tool on this page falls into one of two buckets, and picking the wrong bucket is the most expensive mistake you can make. A self-serve tool is software you rent and operate. A managed agent is a service someone runs for you. Ignore the marketing labels and match your real situation:

  • Fewer than five enquiries a week. Buy nothing. Reply personally and fast. A WhatsApp tool here is a solution looking for a problem.
  • You have someone to run a tool, and a tight budget. A self-serve platform. AiSensy or ManyChat for marketing, Wati or Interakt for a WhatsApp inbox, Gallabox for lead capture.
  • A team answers thousands of chats a month across channels. respond.io, or SleekFlow if you want an omnichannel suite with a local Singapore office.
  • You have engineers and want full control. Build it on Twilio.
  • Each enquiry could be worth hundreds or thousands of dollars, replies go out late, and nobody will run a tool. A managed AI sales agent like 41 Closer.
  • The bot must quote from live inventory or work inside internal systems no tool can reach. A custom build, typically S$15,000 to S$50,000, from an AI development company.

The most common failure mode in Singapore is not picking the wrong tool. It is buying a good tool and assigning nobody to run it. Six months later the subscription is still billing and the bot still says "We will get back to you." Decide who operates the thing before you decide which thing. New to all this? WhatsApp marketing and broadcast platforms (self-serve)

These tools are built to start conversations at scale: broadcasts, campaigns, comment-to-DM flows, and FAQ answers. They are the cheapest way in, and your team builds and runs the flows.

AiSensy: best for low-cost WhatsApp marketing and broadcasts

AiSensy is a self-serve WhatsApp platform built on the official WhatsApp Business API, and it says 210,000 businesses across 68 countries use it. It is one of the cheapest ways to run broadcasts and campaigns at scale. Its AI chatbot is a paid add-on from about 3,500 rupees a month. Your team runs it.

Two things to note before you sign up. First, AiSensy prices in Indian rupees, with a separate page for buyers outside India, so a Singapore buyer should confirm the current figure and currency. Second, the AI sits on top of the base subscription as an add-on, so budget for both plus Meta's message fees. We put AiSensy head to head with the managed approach in 41 Closer vs AiSensy vs Interakt.

ManyChat: best for Instagram, TikTok and WhatsApp marketing flows

ManyChat automates marketing conversations across Instagram, TikTok, Messenger, and WhatsApp, and reports over one million users. There is a free plan without WhatsApp, and paid plans start at US$14 a month. It is excellent at turning comments into DMs, but it is built for marketing, not for quoting or closing complex sales.

ManyChat restructured its plans in 2026. WhatsApp automation sits on its Pro tier, which starts around US$29 a month billed annually and scales with your active contact count, so a big audience raises the bill, and its AI is a paid add-on. It is one of seven channels here rather than a WhatsApp-first tool. We compare it with the WhatsApp-first options in 41 Closer vs Wati vs ManyChat.

WhatsApp team inbox and sales suites (self-serve)

These platforms connect a chatbot and a shared team inbox to the WhatsApp Business API, so a team can answer, broadcast, and track leads from one screen. All three below are software you operate. Meta's per-conversation fees apply on top of every subscription.

Wati: best for a WhatsApp-first team inbox and broadcasts

Wati is a WhatsApp-first engagement platform on the official Business API, used by more than 16,000 businesses. You get a shared team inbox, broadcasts, no-code chatbots, and Wati AI agents. Plans start at US$59 a month billed annually, plus Meta's message fees. Your team builds and maintains the flows.

The extras add up: extra users, AI co-pilot credits, and add-ons sit on top of the base plan, and there is a 7-day free trial with no setup fee. Wati sells you the toolbox, not the outcome, so it rewards a team with someone to own it. The full trade-offs are in 41 Closer vs Wati vs ManyChat.

Interakt: best for WhatsApp plus Instagram with a light CRM

Interakt, from Jio Haptik, combines WhatsApp and Instagram with marketing, support, and a light sales CRM in one tool, and says 50,000 businesses use it. There is a free Instagram-only Starter plan; paid WhatsApp plans start at US$55 a month. Its WhatsApp AI Agents are a paid add-on. Your team runs it.

Backed by Reliance Jio, Interakt is a solid pick if you want WhatsApp and Instagram in one login with a support hub and pipeline attached. Note that pricing differs across its India and international pages and the AI Agents add-on is charged separately, so price your real setup before committing. See it compared in 41 Closer vs AiSensy vs Interakt.

Gallabox: best for WhatsApp lead capture and a shared sales inbox

Gallabox is a self-serve WhatsApp platform on the official Business API, focused on capturing and qualifying leads and working them from a shared team inbox. It includes WhatsApp chatbots and AI reply tools. Pricing is in Indian rupees, from about 2,399 rupees a month billed annually, with US dollar and AED options.

Gallabox leans into sales team workflows: lead assignment, drip nurture, and no-code chatbots that hand off to a human agent. Like the other India-based tools here, it prices mainly in rupees, so a Singapore buyer should confirm the current figure and currency. It is a strong low-cost pick when the job is capturing enquiries and distributing them to a team that will work them.

Multi-channel platforms for bigger teams (self-serve)

When WhatsApp is one of several channels and you have staff to run an inbox at scale, these two are the strongest options available in Singapore. Both include AI agents you configure yourself.

respond.io: best multi-channel inbox for bigger teams

respond.io unifies WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, email, and calls into one inbox with AI Agents that qualify leads and book appointments. Over 10,000 businesses use it, and it is rated 4.8 on G2. Plans start at US$79 a month, with AI included from US$159. Best for teams with an ops person to run it.

Headquartered in Kuala Lumpur and backed by a US$62.5 million Series B, respond.io is built for volume across channels, and its AI agents can update CRM fields, recommend products, and escalate to a human. It is software you operate, so it rewards a dedicated owner and punishes teams that do not give it one. We put it head to head with the managed approach in 41 Closer vs respond.io vs SleekFlow.

SleekFlow: best omnichannel suite for Asia brands with a Singapore office

SleekFlow is an omnichannel messaging suite for consumer brands, with a Singapore office and an official Meta partnership. Its AgentFlow agents handle inbound sales, follow-up, and analytics across WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok, SMS, and email. There is a free plan; paid AI plans start at HK$1,199 a month for three users.

If a vendor with people in Singapore matters to you, SleekFlow is the closest fit among the platforms, and it reports over one million conversations powered daily. Two things to watch: prices display in Hong Kong dollars even on the Singapore site, and its AgentFlow pricing is not published separately, so ask for it. It is compared alongside respond.io in 41 Closer vs respond.io vs SleekFlow.

Build your own: developer platforms

If you have engineers, you can skip the platforms entirely and build exactly what you want on raw infrastructure. Cheaper per message, and far more work.

Twilio: best for developers building their own WhatsApp AI

Twilio is a developer platform, or CPaaS, that gives your engineers programmable APIs and access to the WhatsApp Business API. You pay per message, US$0.005 each plus Meta's fees, with no platform subscription. There is nothing to use until your team builds it, so it fits companies with engineers to spare.

The message fee is not the real cost. The real cost is the engineering time to build the conversation logic, connect your catalogue, wire in the AI, and keep it running. If you have that team, Twilio is superb value and you own the whole system. If you do not, those costs are very real and usually exceed a managed subscription, they just do not show on the pricing page. We lay out the build-versus-buy maths in 41 Closer vs Twilio.

Red flags to avoid when buying a WhatsApp AI tool

  • Nobody owns it. If you cannot name the person who will run and maintain the tool, do not buy self-serve software. Buy managed or buy nothing.
  • You cannot talk to a live bot before paying. Any serious vendor can show you a working agent, not slides. Ours answers +65 8012 4848; hold every vendor to the same bar.
  • The quote hides Meta's WhatsApp fees. Every WhatsApp tool pays Meta per-conversation charges on top. A vendor who does not mention them is hiding your real cost.
  • Prices in a currency you do not use. Several strong tools quote in Indian rupees or Hong Kong dollars. Confirm the figure and currency in Singapore dollars before you sign.
  • "Unlimited AI" with undefined limits. AI replies cost the vendor money per message. Unlimited usually means a fair-use clause you have not read.
  • No human handoff. A bot that cannot pass a hot lead or an angry customer to a person will cost you sales and reviews.
  • It chats but cannot quote. If the bot cannot use your real price list, it is a brochure with a typing indicator.
  • The AI is an add-on you did not budget for. On several platforms here the AI sits on top of the base plan. Price the whole thing, not the headline tier.

Can AI reply to WhatsApp automatically?

Yes. Connected through the WhatsApp Business API, an AI agent can read an incoming message and reply in seconds, at any hour, without a person. Good ones answer questions, quote prices, and follow up when a lead goes quiet. The difference between tools is what the AI can do: some only answer FAQs, while a managed sales agent like 41 Closer quotes from your real price list and works the conversation toward a sale, handing off to a human when needed.

What is the difference between a WhatsApp chatbot and an AI sales agent?

A WhatsApp chatbot answers questions from a script or knowledge base, then usually hands the chat to your team. Do I need the WhatsApp Business API?

For business automation, yes. The free WhatsApp Business app is fine for replying by hand on one phone, but any AI, chatbot, broadcast, or shared team inbox runs on the official WhatsApp Business API. Every platform here connects through it, and Meta charges per-conversation fees on top of your software.
