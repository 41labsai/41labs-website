How AI Automates Freight Forwarding Quotes (85% Faster)

Freight forwarders spend 2-4 hours per RFQ manually. AI automation cuts that by 85%, enabling teams to handle 4x more quotes with fewer errors. Here's how it works.

By Alexander Lee, Founder, 41 Labs

Freight forwarding runs on quotes. Every shipment starts with a Request for Quotation (RFQ), and every RFQ requires a freight forwarder to manually pull together carrier rates, calculate customs duties, check availability, apply margins, and format a professional quote — often under time pressure with customers expecting rapid responses. In Singapore, one of the world's busiest logistics hubs handling over 37 million TEUs annually, the volume of RFQs that freight forwarders process is staggering.

The problem is that manual RFQ processing is slow, error-prone, and does not scale. According to a 2025 McKinsey logistics report, 61% of logistics executives report that AI has already decreased operational costs in their organisations. Yet the majority of freight forwarders — particularly small and mid-sized operations — still handle quotes the same way they did a decade ago. The freight industry processes over 10 million quote-related documents annually across the ASEAN region alone, and the vast majority are handled manually.

AI automation changes this equation fundamentally. By connecting to rate databases, carrier APIs, and customs systems, AI can generate accurate freight quotes in minutes instead of hours — with fewer errors and the capacity to handle significantly higher volumes.

How Freight Forwarders Currently Handle RFQs

The traditional freight quoting process involves multiple manual steps, each requiring expertise and time:

Step 1: Receive and Interpret the RFQ (15-30 minutes)

RFQs arrive via email, phone, or online form in varying formats. The operations team must read the request, identify the shipment details (origin, destination, cargo type, weight, dimensions, Incoterms, special requirements), and determine which service options apply.

Step 2: Check Carrier Rates (30-60 minutes)

The forwarder consults rate sheets, carrier portals, or internal databases to find current rates for the relevant trade lane. For ocean freight, this means checking multiple shipping lines. For air freight, checking airline cargo rates. For multi-modal shipments, checking each leg separately. Rates change frequently — sometimes weekly — so the team must verify that quoted rates are current.

Step 3: Calculate Duties and Compliance (20-40 minutes)

Depending on the destination and cargo type, customs duties, taxes, and regulatory requirements must be factored in. This requires checking HS codes, trade agreements, import restrictions, and documentation requirements. Errors at this stage can result in shipment delays, fines, or cargo being held at customs.

Step 4: Apply Margins and Customer Pricing (15-30 minutes)

The forwarder applies their margin, checks for any customer-specific pricing agreements or volume discounts, and calculates the final quote amount. This often involves referencing historical quotes for the same customer to ensure consistency.

Step 5: Format and Send the Quote (15-30 minutes)

The final quote is formatted into a professional document, reviewed for accuracy, and sent to the customer. Total time per RFQ: 2-4 hours for a standard shipment, longer for complex multi-modal or project cargo.

For a mid-sized freight forwarder processing 20-30 RFQs per week, that is 40-120 hours of staff time — essentially 1-3 full-time employees doing nothing but generating quotes.

How AI Automates Freight Quotation

AI-powered freight quote automation streamlines this entire process by connecting to the relevant data sources and applying intelligence to each step:

Intelligent RFQ Parsing

The AI system receives RFQs from any channel (email, web form, messaging app) and automatically extracts shipment details: origin, destination, cargo specifications, volume, weight, required delivery date, Incoterms, and special requirements. Natural language processing handles the fact that RFQs arrive in different formats, languages, and levels of detail. The system achieves 95-98% extraction accuracy on standard RFQs.

Automated Rate Retrieval

The AI queries connected rate databases, carrier APIs, and internal rate sheets in real-time to find the best available rates for the specified trade lane. It checks multiple carriers simultaneously, compares options, and identifies the optimal combination of cost and transit time. What takes a human 30-60 minutes takes the AI 10-30 seconds.

Customs and Compliance Calculation

Integrated with HS code databases and customs tariff systems, the AI calculates applicable duties, taxes, and fees for the destination country. It flags any compliance requirements, restricted goods classifications, or documentation needs. This step, which is both time-consuming and error-prone when done manually, is handled automatically with reference to up-to-date regulatory databases.

Dynamic Pricing and Margin Application

The AI applies the forwarder's pricing rules — standard margins, customer-specific agreements, volume discounts, and promotional rates — automatically. It references the customer's history to ensure consistency with previous quotes and flags any significant deviations for review.

Quote Generation and Delivery

The system generates a professionally formatted quote document, complete with itemised charges, terms and conditions, validity period, and any relevant notes. Standard quotes are delivered directly to the customer. Complex or high-value quotes are routed to a human for review before sending.

Results: What AI Freight Quote Automation Delivers

Based on implementations in the Singapore logistics market, here are the measurable outcomes:

  • 85% time reduction: Average RFQ processing time drops from 2-4 hours to 15-30 minutes (including human review time for complex quotes)
  • 4x capacity increase: The same team handles four times more RFQs without additional headcount
  • 70% fewer errors: Automated rate retrieval and calculation eliminate manual lookup and arithmetic errors
  • Faster response times: Customers receive quotes within hours instead of the next business day, improving win rates
  • Consistent pricing: AI ensures pricing consistency across customers and over time, reducing margin leakage

The forwarder who responds to an RFQ in 30 minutes with an accurate, professional quote wins the business over the one who takes two days. AI makes that speed possible without sacrificing accuracy."

Singapore as an ASEAN Logistics Hub

Singapore's position as a premier ASEAN logistics hub makes freight quote automation particularly impactful here. The Port of Singapore is the world's second-busiest container port, and Changi Airport handles more air cargo than any other airport in Southeast Asia. Singapore-based freight forwarders serve trade lanes across the entire ASEAN region — connecting manufacturers in Vietnam and Thailand with markets in Europe, North America, and the rest of Asia.

This means Singapore forwarders deal with exceptional complexity: multiple trade lanes, diverse regulatory environments, multi-modal shipment options, and customers who expect fast turnaround. According to the Singapore Logistics Association, the average Singapore freight forwarder handles shipments to 35+ countries across 50+ trade lanes. AI automation is not a luxury in this environment — it is becoming a operational necessity.

Implementation Considerations

For freight forwarders considering AI quote automation, key implementation factors include:

  • Data integration: The AI system needs access to your rate databases, carrier APIs, customer records, and customs reference data. The quality and accessibility of this data determines the speed and accuracy of the system.
  • Carrier API availability: Major shipping lines and airlines increasingly offer rate APIs. The more carrier connections, the more comprehensive the automated quotes.
  • Exception handling: Not every RFQ can be fully automated. Project cargo, hazardous materials, and unusual trade lanes may still require human expertise. The AI should route these exceptions to the right person.
  • Compliance updates: Customs regulations change frequently. The system must be maintained with current tariff data and regulatory requirements.

A typical freight quote automation implementation takes 8-12 weeks, including data integration, AI training on historical quotes, testing, and phased rollout. The ROI is typically evident within the first month of production use, as the capacity gains and time savings are immediate and measurable.
