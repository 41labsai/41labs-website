The DIY tool trap nobody mentions

ManyChat has a free plan. Wati starts at US$59 a month. 41 Closer starts at S$690 a month. If price were the whole story, this article would be one line long.

Here is the part the pricing pages do not show. A self-serve tool is a box of parts. Someone in your company has to connect the WhatsApp Business API, design the conversation flows, write every reply, test them, watch the inbox daily, and fix what breaks. Then keep improving it every month, because the first version is never right.

If you have that person, self-serve is genuinely good value. Thousands of businesses run Wati and ManyChat well, and both give you a lot of capability for the money.

If you do not have that person, a familiar pattern plays out. The tool gets half set up over a free weekend. The chatbot learns to answer three FAQs. Then a customer asks a real question, the bot has no answer, and your staff go back to typing replies by hand. The subscription keeps billing. Nobody cancels it, and nobody uses it.

That is not a flaw in Wati or ManyChat. It is the adoption pattern of every self-serve tool, from CRMs to email software. So the honest question is not which tool is best. It is who in your company will run it. We break down that decision in more detail in our guide to a managed WhatsApp AI agent versus a DIY chatbot.

What is Wati?

Wati is a self-serve customer engagement platform built on the official WhatsApp Business API. Your team uses it to build no-code chatbots, send broadcast campaigns, and manage a shared team inbox. It also offers AI agents under its Wati AI layer. Wati says it serves 16,000+ businesses worldwide. Plans start at US$59 a month billed annually.

What is ManyChat?

ManyChat is a self-serve chat automation tool used by creators, small businesses, and brands. It is strongest on Instagram, TikTok, and Messenger, where it automates comment replies and DMs, and it supports WhatsApp on its Pro plan. ManyChat reports over 1 million users. There is a free plan, and WhatsApp automation starts at US$29 a month billed annually.

What is 41 Closer?

41 Closer is a managed AI sales agent for WhatsApp, built and run by 41 Labs in Singapore. It answers every enquiry in seconds, 24/7, quotes real prices from your own catalogue, follows up every lead, and closes the sale. There is no software for your team to learn. Plans start at S$690 a month.

When Wati or ManyChat is the better choice

Plenty of businesses should buy the tool, not the service. Be honest with yourself about which list you are on.

  • You have a capable ops person with time. Someone who enjoys building flows, testing them, and improving them monthly. In those hands, both tools return far more than they cost.
  • Your budget is tight. ManyChat has a free plan and its WhatsApp-capable Pro plan is US$29 a month billed annually. Wati starts at US$59 a month billed annually with a 7-day free trial and no setup fee. 41 Closer cannot compete on entry price, and I will not pretend otherwise.
  • Your use case is simple. Answering the same ten FAQs, sending order updates, or broadcasting a monthly promo. Scripted flows handle this well. You do not need a sales agent for it.
  • Your audience lives on Instagram or TikTok. ManyChat is the clear pick. Turning comments into DM conversations is its home turf, and it is an official Meta Business Partner and TikTok Marketing Partner.
  • You want human agents in a shared inbox. Wati is built for this, with assignment, routing, and reporting for a support or sales team that answers chats themselves.

If two or more of those describe you, buy the tool. It is the right call, and you will not miss what 41 Closer does. For the wider field of options, see our guide to the best AI chatbots for business in Singapore, or a similar three-way breakdown of 41 Closer vs AiSensy vs Interakt.

How much does Wati cost?

As of July 3, 2026, Wati lists three plans: Growth at US$59 a month, Pro at US$119 a month, and Business at US$279 a month, all billed annually. Billed monthly they are US$69, US$149, and US$349.
