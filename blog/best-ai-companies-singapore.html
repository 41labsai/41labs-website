How we evaluated

We scored every company on five things that matter when you are spending real money on AI:

Production track record. Does the company have systems running today, with numbers attached? Live systems beat case-study PDFs.

Delivery speed. Weeks or months to a live system? For most SMEs, a system that ships this quarter beats a better system that ships next year.

Pricing transparency. Do they publish real numbers, or hide everything behind a sales call?

Grant support. Can they help you use EDG or PSG funding? In Singapore this can halve your cost. Our EDG grant guide for AI projects covers what qualifies.

Specialisation. A voice AI specialist beats a generalist for voice. A builder beats a trainer for production systems. The right pick depends on your problem.

Every fact in this guide comes from each company's own public website, checked on July 2, 2026. Where a company does not publish a number, we say so rather than guess.

How to choose between them

Three questions sort out most shortlists.

1. What exactly is the problem? If one expensive workflow is eating hours, like quotes, documents or WhatsApp enquiries, pick a focused AI development company in Singapore: 41 Labs, Analytico or NexStack. If you need a platform or app built, look at Techtiq or OTG Lab. If it is phone calls at volume, Wiz.AI. If your team needs skills and independent advice first, an AI consulting partner like GetAIReady or AIMS fits. If it is many departments at once, Tangentia or ABeam.

2. What is your real budget after grants? A S$20,000 project can cost S$10,000 out of pocket with EDG support, and several firms on this list help with applications. Work out the numbers with our AI consulting cost guide before you take a single sales call. Knowing the market rate changes the conversation.

3. What does success look like in 90 days? Write it down in hours saved or dollars won. Then ask each vendor to commit to it. The ones who answer in plain numbers are the ones who have shipped before. The ones who answer in frameworks have not.

Red flags to avoid

Five warning signs that show up repeatedly in the Singapore AI market:

1. No production case studies. If a vendor cannot name one system with quantified results that still runs today, they are selling theory.

2. Vague pricing. "Let's discuss on a call" often means "we price on what we think you can pay." Transparent vendors give ranges upfront.

3. Strategy first, system never. If the first deliverable is a roadmap document rather than anything that runs, you may pay twice: once for the deck, once for the build.

4. The senior person disappears after signing. If the founder or partner pitches you and juniors deliver, quality drops. Ask who exactly will build your system.

5. No talk of failure modes. AI systems fail in specific ways: hallucinations, edge cases, drift. A vendor who cannot explain the human fallback has not deployed enough systems to know.

A focused custom AI system takes 4 to 8 weeks with a boutique builder like 41 Labs. Wiz.AI reports standard voice agent deployments going live from day 2. Large enterprise programmes with firms like ABeam Consulting or Tangentia typically run several months to more than a year because of scope and governance.

Should I hire a local boutique AI company or a global consultancy?

Match the firm to the size of the problem. If you need one high-value process fixed fast, a boutique like 41 Labs, Analytico or NexStack will be quicker and far cheaper. If you are transforming several departments at once with formal governance and a six-figure budget, shortlist ABeam Consulting, Tangentia or another large firm. If your aim is to automate one repetitive workflow end to end, an AI automation agency in Singapore is the category to shortlist.

What should I ask an AI company before hiring them?

Ask four things. How many systems have you shipped, and how many still run today? Who actually builds my system, and will I speak to them? What will this save me in hours or dollars, in plain numbers? And can you show me a working demo on my own data before I pay? Any strong vendor can answer all four.
