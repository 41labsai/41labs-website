## Speed-to-lead (hero argument, [SOURCED])
**78% of buyers buy from the dealer who responds FIRST.** 82% expect a reply <10min. <5min = 100x more likely to connect; >30min = 21x less likely to close. Average dealership takes **1hr 38min** → buyer has heard from 3–5 dealers. **58.9% of qualified car leads buy within 3 days.** [Demand Local, giosg, DealerPulse]

## Objections (state + knock down)
1. "Car selling needs a human, can't negotiate with a bot." → it qualifies + books the test drive; never haggles nett price; you close in person. [best practice: bot qualifies, human negotiates]
2. "Trade-in/loan are complex/negotiated." → collects inputs (car for trade-in, budget, financing need), hands you a warm buyer; never commits a number.
3. "A bot will give a wrong price and I'm stuck." (Real: a US Chevy dealer bot was tricked into 'selling' a $70k SUV for $1.) → managed + guard-railed; never quotes a final price or makes an offer; escalates to you. This is the point of *managed* vs a raw ChatGPT bot.
