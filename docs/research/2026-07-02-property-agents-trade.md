## Deal value (commission)
Convention (CEA sets no rate): HDB resale 1–2% (2% seller side standard); private resale 1–2%; new launch 2–3% (developer pays, buyer $0). [SOURCED: 99.co, NewPropertyLaunches, LovelyHomes]
Per-deal $ [GUESS on real prices]: $600k HDB @2% = **$12,000**; $1.5M condo @1.5–2% = **$22,500–$30,000**; $2M new launch @3% = **$60,000**. Frame as "a deal is worth $X", not "you earn X". Page uses **$12,000–$60,000**.

## Leads + volume
Channels: PropertyGuru (#1, pushes WhatsApp Lead Alerts), 99.co (#2, leads from $0.26 each via WhatsApp), EdgeProp/SRX, FB/IG, video. [SOURCED]
Volume: featured listing ~2–10 leads/mo; 6 listings ≈ 15–60 enquiries/mo, each a separate WhatsApp thread, often after hours. Agents pay per lead and don't own buyer data → wasting a lead wastes cash.

## Speed-to-lead (the hero argument, all [SOURCED], US/NAR-origin — attribute "industry studies")
**78% of buyers work with the FIRST agent who responds.** Reply <5min = 21x more likely to convert; <5min vs 30min = 100x more likely to reach them. Average agent takes ~917 min (15+ hrs); 41% never reply, only 9% within 5 min.

## Objections (state + knock down)
1. "Relationship business, AI sounds robotic." → managed, trained on your voice; does first-touch + qualify + book, hands you the warm lead for the relationship.
2. "Every client/unit different, can't handle nuance/nego." → answers facts + qualifies; never negotiates or commits price; sensitive stuff ("can nego?") routes to you.
3. "CEA rules — representation, no dual representation (a $9k fine case)." → only does first-response, info, viewing-booking; never signs agreements, represents a second party, or collects commission. Position as inbox assistant, not your licence.

## SG context 2026
